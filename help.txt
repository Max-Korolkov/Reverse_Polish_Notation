Reverse Polish notation (RPN), also known as Polish postfix notation or simply postfix notation, is a mathematical notation in which operators follow their operands.
It does not need any parentheses as long as each operator has a fixed number of operands.
For example: 2 3 + 5 -, and the answer to this expression will be 0.

To add variable to memory, type in the following expression:
[Variable name] [It's value] =

Available commands:

LISTVAR
Lists all known variables in console.

CLRVAR
Deletes known variables from memory.

ESCAPE
Exits the program.
